Introduction to Project 3

This is a game that Yun Che and I are working on. It is called The Drift Battle. It is a 2-player game that which you can race with your friends. Both players will be battling against each other to get the fastet lap. Players are able to use nitrogen to speed through corners and also hit each other off the track during the race.

Inspiration
p5 Libraries

p5.play: there will be animation on the user's and opponent's car.

p5.sound: there will be background music while playing so as the sound effect on the cars.

p5.dom: there will be a slider that control the volume of the background music and the sound effect from the cars, so as the road animation.

Arguments and context of how your project attempts to address the Interface Contexts.

1. Interface as Formal System, Language and Role-Playing Device

- The movement keys for player-1 will be ↑→↓← and the player-2 will be WASD. Both players will be compete with the best movement controls.

- The background will be running straight or left&right turns continuously and randomly.

2. Simulation

- The game is representation and simulation of car drifting and racing in the real world. Two players will be racing for the fastest laps.

- In professional drifting, there is a thing called drift tandems which both cars drift very closely at the same pace.

Skeumorphism vs Flat Design

- We will be using 2D modern race car and the common 2D racing road.
