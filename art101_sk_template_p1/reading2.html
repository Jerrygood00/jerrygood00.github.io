Q2. From the Manovich reading, what is the difference between visualization and mapping?
“Visualization for the situations when quantified data which by itself is not visual – the output of meteorological sensors, stock market behaviors, the set of addresses describing the trajectory of a message through a computer network, and so on – is transformed into a visual representation. Laura Frick

Catagories:
A. Crowd-Sourcing B. Data as Process and Exploration D. Constructing Identity E. Self Surveillance G. Creating Data Narratives

Summary:
Lauren Frick is a data artist, she uses data to explore more and experiments on with self-tracking. One of her the artworks she created was she used data from Zeo, a an EEG sensor that measures how well you sleep. The results she got formed a pattern and noticed she doesn’t get much deep sleep but mostly have ainly has busy sleeps and is awake a lot. This was a hidden part of her, something she hasn’t seen about herself. She created a rule system with colors like red represents deep sleep, while yellow is REM sleep. She put this work into an art show and got noticed by the head of Zeo Inc., asking what he can do to help . so Frick asked for more data so she got data from the workers of Zeo so she compare her records with those. The color purple represents deep sleep, orange color represents waking up, the fingerprint results that male workers at the age 46 don’t get a lot of deep sleep while females at age 26 gets more deep sleep than males at around the same age. She believes that data tracking is to understand some part of us that we didn’t know about, like our other self. Patterns can tells us about ourselves and what makes us who we are or who we wanna bee.

Stefanie Posavec

Catagories:
B. Data as Process and Exploration C. Data as Portraiture G. Creating Data Narratives

Summary:
Stefanie Posavec is a designer, data is her favorite material for working. Her work ranges from data visualization and information design to entrusting various clients with data art. Her personal work focuses mainly on non-traditional data representations obtained from linguistic, literary, or scientific topics, often using manual methods. She likes to manually count and calculate with her reliable mechanical pencil She works books and data, and everything in between. She mentions what is date illustrator in her speech. First, someone who represents data in order to communicate additional(subjective) messages that are not found in the data itself. Second, someone who uses data to build images where the form is as important as the data. Third, someone who uses data to arrive at design solution, where the data is not the main message. Forth, someone who uses the process of data-gathering to create meaningful visuals for a design. Fifth, someone who looks for data they can use as a lens to highlight a subject from a new angle. Furthermore, she shows her project for each point, and explain the theme of her project, for example, she translates a book into a flower project, and the leaf means the chapter she reads, and the branch means the paragraphs. She uses data create many interesting project, and she is enjoying translating books by using data.

Nicholas Felton

Catagories:
C. Data as Portraiture E. Self Surveillance G. Creating Data Narratives

Summary:
Nicholas Felton was a graphic designer who later became very interested in the power of number. His intention was to transform data into meaningful stories. He used himself as an experiment, marked his behavior and significant moment every single day, then calculated them after a year using processing, so the result turned out to be a visually satisfying graphical art summarizing his past year, for example: what he often ate in a day, how often he goes to the gym, how many places he traveled in the past year, how many movies have he watched, etc. It’s possible to find out his favorite restaurant, his favorite song or movie among the results. What Nicholas Felton did is he put the data into processing, then turned it into visualization. The result is made of data, but it’s more persuasive than data itself. He used this method to record his life, to tell story, and to better know himself. He truly respect the power of number.
